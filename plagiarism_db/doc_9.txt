Biophilic design represents a paradigm shift in how we conceive of urban living. Rather than viewing nature as a decorative secondary element, this architectural philosophy integrates biological patterns directly into the built environment. By incorporating living walls, natural ventilation systems, and organic fractals into high-density housing, developers can significantly reduce resident stress and improve overall cognitive function. It is not merely about "greening" a city; it is about recognizing that human well-being is biologically dependent on a continuous connection to the natural world.As global urbanization accelerates, the traditional "concrete jungle" model has led to a spike in environmental stressors, ranging from poor air quality to social isolation.
